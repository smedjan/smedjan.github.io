Back the forge

Smedjan is one engineer's engine — MIT-licensed, with no company and no venture money behind it. Sponsorship buys GPU hours and the time to keep building open infrastructure that lets anyone own their own language models.

// 01

Why it matters

the case

Owning a language model shouldn't require a Python stack, a cloud account, or somebody else's API. Smedjan exists so a person, a lab, or a company can train, run, and own a model end to end — on their own hardware, disconnected from the world if they want. Independent infrastructure only stays independent if it's funded independently.

What your money buys

GPU and Apple-Silicon hardware hours for development and benchmarking, kernel and backend work, documentation, and the maintainer's time — kept free of any framework or vendor you don't control.

What you get back

A stack you can read end to end, no lock-in, portable checkpoints, and a maintainer who answers. Sponsors shape what gets forged next.
