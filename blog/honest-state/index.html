What's done, and what isn't

A new engine is easy to oversell. Here is the real state of Smedjan — the same map you will find in the docs — so you can decide what to trust it with.

What trains today: the full pipeline — tokenizer, data prep, pretraining, knowledge distillation, supervised fine-tuning, DPO alignment, quantization, and export — on both Metal and CUDA, with a portable checkpoint format. The selective state-space (Mamba-2/SSD) and linear-attention mixers train. AdamW, Muon, and the hybrid optimizer all work.

What is experimental: the RWKV mixer is wired for development but does not converge yet, and block-sparse attention is experimental. Use them to explore, not to ship.

What is on the roadmap: safetensors import is F32-only today (export works now); GGUF export covers f32 and q8_0; faithful HuggingFace inference parity (config.json → model, bf16 loading) and CUDA backward parity for a few specialized kernels are still being completed.

I would rather you find this list here than in a failed run. The full, always-current version lives in the troubleshooting & FAQ page. If the honest version excites you rather than worries you, the repository is open.

— Andrei
